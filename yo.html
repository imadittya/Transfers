To preserve and promote the rich tradition of Bidri craft, empowering artisans and ensuring sustainable livelihoods, our mission is to uphold the legacy of this exquisite art form while adapting to the demands of the modern world. Bidriware, renowned for its intricate silver inlays on a blackened alloy base, is not just a craft but a cultural heritage that represents the confluence of Persian and Indian artistry.

Our Heritage

Bidri craft of Karnataka stands as a testament to the rich cultural heritage and artistic prowess of the region. Rooted in the historic city of Bidar, this exquisite craft tradition embodies centuries of skillful craftsmanship and cultural fusion. With its origins tracing back to the 14th century, Bidri craft flourished under the patronage of the Bahamani Sultanate, blending Persian techniques with indigenous Indian artistry. Characterized by its distinctive blackened alloy adorned with intricate silver inlays, Bidriware reflects the convergence of diverse cultural influences, including Persian, Mughal, and Deccan aesthetics. Each meticulously crafted piece, be it a vase, box, or ornament, narrates tales of skilled artisans who have passed down their intricate techniques through generations.
