Bidriware Crafts of Karnataka

Bidriware is a traditional metal handicraft that originated in Bidar, Karnataka, India, during the rule of the Bahamani Sultans. It involves the craft of inlaying silver or gold into blackened alloy of zinc and copper, giving it a striking contrast and intricate designs.

The craft is characterized by its unique matte finish and fine patterns, often depicting flora and fauna. Bidriware artifacts include vases, plates, bowls, jewelry boxes, and various decorative items.

Today, Bidriware craftsmen continue to preserve this ancient art form, creating exquisite pieces that are admired both nationally and internationally.
